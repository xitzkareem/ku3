
\section{Different representations of integers}


\subsection{Write the binary number $(110)_2$ in decimal notation}
Binary notation refers to the number system of base 2, where as the decimal notation refers to base 10. I will start by expanding $(110)_2 = 1*2^2+1*2^1+0*2^0=6$, now I will try to write the number 6 in decimal notation. To do that I will use the equation $$m=q_0 \cdot b +r_0...$$
 $$...m=q_n \cdot b +r_n$$
where : $m: \text{is the number we are trying to rewrite, } b:\text{the base}$, we continue running the recursion process until $q_n= 0$ 
$$6=0\cdot 10+6 $$ since $q_0=0$ we stop and now we have $$(110)_2=(6)_{10}$$

\subsection{Write the decimal number $110$ in binary notation}
Following the steps of the previous question,  we have 
$$(110)_{10}=1 \cdot 10^2+1 \cdot 10^1 + 0 \cdot 10^0=110$$
to convert the number 110 to base 2 we use the equation : 
$$110= 55 \cdot2+0$$
$$55= 27\cdot 2+ 1$$
$$27= 13\cdot 2 +1$$
$$13=  6\cdot 2+1$$
$$6=3*2+0$$
$$3= 1\cdot 2+1$$
$$1= 0 \cdot2 +1$$

Then we have that 
$$(110)_{10}= (1101110)_2$$
\subsection{Write the octal number $(110)_8$ in decimal notation}
$$(110)_{8}= 1 \cdot 8^2+1 \cdot 8^1 + 0 \cdot8^0 = 64+8+0=72$$
$$72= 7 \cdot 10+2$$
$$7= 0 \cdot 10+7$$
hence $$(110)_8=(72)_{10}$$
